# My Story, Before We Talk: Jessica Argonaut

*Prepared for my cardiology visit on June 12, 2026 · DOB March 14, 1985*

## What I'm most worried about

I want to be honest at the start: I'm scared of being told this is anxiety again. Two years ago, when the palpitations first showed up, I was sent home with a pamphlet about stress, and for eighteen months I believed it. Then the episode in February happened — I was carrying laundry up one flight of stairs and had to sit down on the landing because ==my heart was pounding so hard I could feel it in my teeth==. My smartwatch said 162. I sat there for almost ten minutes before it settled.

My father had his first bypass at 54. He is 68 now and doing well, but I watched what those years of "let's just keep an eye on it" cost him, and ==I do not want to wait until something breaks to be taken seriously==. I'm 41. I have a nine-year-old who asks why Mom stops on the stairs.

> I'm not asking you to find something wrong with me. I'm asking you to look hard enough that, if you tell me I'm fine, I can actually believe it.

## What I've noticed

I've been keeping notes since March, because I know "sometimes my heart races" isn't useful to you. Here's the pattern as best I can tell:

- Episodes happen two or three times a week, usually mid-morning or after climbing stairs, lasting two to ten minutes.
- They start abruptly — like a switch, not a ramp — and end the same way. That suddenness is the part that frightens me most.
- Coffee doesn't seem to matter; I quit for three weeks in April and ==the episodes kept coming on schedule anyway==.
- During episodes I get lightheaded and my vision narrows a little, but I have never fully fainted.
- My watch has logged peaks of 158–171 bpm at rest. I've exported the recordings and can share them.

I've also gained about twelve pounds since starting the new job in January, I sleep badly (my husband says I snore now, which is new), and my ankles are puffy by evening a couple of days a week. Maybe those are separate stories. Maybe they aren't. You'll know better than I do.

## What I want from this visit

I want a plan with names and dates on it, even if the plan is "watchful waiting" — as long as we've ruled out the things that shouldn't wait. Specifically, I'm hoping we can talk about whether a monitor longer than 48 hours makes sense, what my father's history means for me, and whether any of my current medications could be contributing. If you give me homework, I will do it. I'm organized to a fault; it's how I cope.

I uploaded my watch ECG strips and my symptom diary to the patient portal ahead of time. The direct link my portal generated is https://portal.examplehealth.org/share/jargonaut/cardiology-prep-2026/recordings-and-diary-export-v2 in case the attachment didn't come through.

Thank you for reading this before we meet. It means I get to spend our fifteen minutes being a person instead of a recitation.
