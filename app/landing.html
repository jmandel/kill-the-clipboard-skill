SMART Health Links · Kill The Clipboard

Share your health record like a boarding pass.

Every clinic asks for the same information — medications, allergies, problems, immunizations — and almost none of them can get it from anywhere except you, typed in fresh. This skill helps your AI agent assemble what matters, tell your story in your own words, and hand it to the front desk as a QR code you control.

How it works

Four steps, no portal sign-in

The same flow whether you prepare the night before or at the front desk: you decide, your agent assembles, the clinic scans, your chart gets it.

1

You choose what to share

Your agent walks you through it in plain language — problems, medications, allergies, immunizations, recent results — plus the story you want your care team to hear. You control every piece.

2

Your agent creates a secure link

Everything is encrypted on your device and published as a SMART Health Link. Treat the QR like a temporary key: it expires, allows limited uses, and you can revoke it any time.

3

The clinic scans your QR

Show it at check-in, or paste the link into a pre-visit form. No account, no portal password, no retyping.

4

It lands in your chart

Participating EHRs save your key clinical history and both PDFs to your chart, labeled as patient-shared. A clinician may review before it joins the active record — that's by design.

What gets shared

One encrypted bundle, three kinds of content

Conformant to the CMS health tech ecosystem's Patient-Shared Health Documents profile, so receiving systems know exactly what to do with it.

FHIR · US Core

Structured records

The data clinics retype today — problems, medications, allergies, immunizations — plus anything else from your record you choose to include. Structured, so it can appear in the same lists your care team already uses.

PDF · LOINC 51855-5

Your story, in your words

What you're most worried about. What's changed. What you want from this visit. Your agent interviews you, drafts it, and nothing ships until you approve the text word for word.

PDF · LOINC 60591-5

A readable summary

Every shared record rendered as a clean, human-readable document — so even a clinic that can't ingest structured data yet gets the complete picture.

Where do the records come from? Anything that produces FHIR JSON. health-skillz is the recommended companion — it connects to your patient portals and exports exactly what this skill selects from.

Private by architecture

Not a policy promise — a design property. The hosting server is structurally unable to read what it stores.

Encrypted on your device

The encryption key is derived on your machine and travels only inside the link you share. It is never sent to the server.

The server stores only ciphertext

Encrypted bytes, link settings, and an access log. No keys, no readable health data, ever.

Every access is logged

Your link page shows who opened your records and when — named by the clinic that fetched them.

You hold the kill switch

Pause, extend, or permanently revoke from your link page. Revoked means the data is deleted, not just hidden.

Honest scope

What this solves — and what it doesn't yet

Helps with the questions every clinic repeats

  • Problems and health concerns
  • Medications
  • Allergies
  • Immunizations
  • Recent results you choose to include
  • Your story and context, in your own words

Doesn't replace, yet

  • Visit-specific questionnaires
  • Consent and billing forms
  • Clinic-specific intake questions
  • Specialty screeners
  • Insurance card capture (coming)

This is progress, not the finish line. A future check-in protocol could let clinics ask your app for exactly what they need — reviewed and approved by you. The July 2026 milestone is a meaningful slice of the clipboard.

Get started

Two ways in

Patients & caregivers

Install the skill, talk to your agent

  1. Download the agent skill (skill.zip) — it comes pre-configured to use this server.
  2. On Claude.ai: Settings → Capabilities → Skills → upload the zip. In Claude Code or another CLI agent: unzip it in your workspace.
  3. Then say:
I installed the kill-the-clipboard skill. Help me prepare a SMART Health Link to share my health records before my next visit.

You'll need your records as FHIR files first — your agent will point you to health-skillz if you don't have them yet.

Developers & health IT

Run it, read it, kick the tires

  1. Source, design record, and test suite: github.com/jmandel/kill-the-clipboard-skill
  2. Run your own server: bun install && bun run server — the skill.zip it vends self-configures to your BASE_URL.
  3. The patient-facing link page lives at /s; links it manages implement the SMART Health Links spec and the KTC profile (U-flag, required expiry, audit).
